# 🔷 **TACTIC: Resource Development**  
In human‑behavioral terms, **Resource Development** represents the criminal preparing tools, relationships, and infrastructure before acting.  
Think of it as:  
- The con artist building a believable persona, props, and social scaffolding  
- The violent actor gathering weapons, allies, or staging areas  

Below are the reinterpretations.

# 🔷 **TACTIC: Initial Access**  
In human‑behavioral terms, **Initial Access** represents the moment a criminal *enters* the victim’s world — socially, physically, or psychologically.

For the **Deceptive Manipulator**, this means:  
- Gaining trust  
- Gaining proximity  
- Gaining legitimacy  

For the **Violent/Aggressive Actor**, this means:  
- Gaining physical entry  
- Gaining control  
- Gaining dominance

# 🔷 **TACTIC: Execution**  
In human‑behavioral terms, **Execution** represents the moment the criminal *initiates the harmful behavior* — the con begins, or the violence starts.

For the **Deceptive Manipulator**, this means:  
- Deploying the lie  
- Triggering the emotional hook  
- Initiating the manipulation sequence  

For the **Violent/Aggressive Actor**, this means:  
- Initiating physical force  
- Beginning the intimidation  
- Taking overt control

# 🔷 **TACTIC: Persistence**  
In human‑behavioral terms, **Persistence** represents the criminal’s effort to *remain in the victim’s environment* — psychologically, socially, or physically — even after the victim might otherwise push them out.

For the **Deceptive Manipulator**, persistence means:  
- Staying relevant  
- Staying trusted  
- Staying embedded  

For the **Violent/Aggressive Actor**, persistence means:  
- Staying in control  
- Staying feared  
- Staying physically present or able to return

# 🔷 **TACTIC: Privilege Escalation**  
In human‑behavioral terms, **Privilege Escalation** represents the criminal’s effort to *increase their authority, influence, or control* over the victim or environment.

For the **Deceptive Manipulator**, this means:  
- Gaining more trust  
- Gaining more decision‑making influence  
- Gaining access to deeper personal or organizational layers  

For the **Violent/Aggressive Actor**, this means:  
- Increasing physical dominance  
- Increasing coercive leverage  
- Gaining control over more spaces, people, or resources
