# 📘 **DETECTION INDICATORS FOR BEHAVIORAL TACTICS**  
## **Installment 1 — Reconnaissance, Resource Development, Initial Access**

---

# 🟦 **1. Reconnaissance — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is studying the target.*





### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- Unusual curiosity about personal details  
- Excessive small talk designed to probe  
- Attempts to map relationships (“Who do you live with?”)  
- Repeated “coincidental” encounters  
- Monitoring social media activity closely  

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- Unknown individuals loitering near home/work  
- Vehicles repeatedly passing or parking nearby  
- Someone watching entrances/exits  
- Sudden interest in building layout or schedules  

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- Feeling “studied” or observed  
- Noticing repeated questions from the same person  
- Feeling like someone is “fishing” for information  

### **Escalation Markers**  
- Probing becomes more targeted  
- Attempts to isolate the victim for deeper questioning  
- Offender begins testing boundaries (emotional or physical)  

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Recon + Resource Development → **pre‑operation staging**  
- Recon + Initial Access → **approach phase**  
- Recon + Discovery → **deep mapping underway**  

---

# 🟦 **2. Resource Development — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is building tools, identities, or infrastructure.*





### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- Sudden creation of new identities or accounts  
- Practiced or rehearsed stories  
- Borrowing legitimacy (“I work with…”)  
- Gathering props (documents, uniforms, tools)  

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- Unknown individuals renting storage units or short‑term spaces  
- Acquisition of tools with no legitimate purpose  
- Use of intermediaries to obtain resources  

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- Feeling like someone is “preparing” for something  
- Noticing inconsistencies in the offender’s identity  
- Seeing props or documents that don’t match their story  

### **Escalation Markers**  
- Offender begins staging items near victim  
- Fake identities become more elaborate  
- Tools or weapons appear in proximity  

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Resource Dev + Initial Access → **operation launch imminent**  
- Resource Dev + Defense Evasion → **concealment infrastructure**  
- Resource Dev + Impact → **planned sabotage**  

---

# 🟦 **3. Initial Access — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender gains their first foothold.*





### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- Sudden friendliness from a stranger  
- False emergencies or urgent requests  
- Attempts to exploit trust or authority  
- Coercive questioning  
- “Accidental” encounters that feel staged  

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- Forced entry signs  
- Items planted near entrances  
- Unknown individuals entering restricted areas  
- Tailgating into secure spaces  

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- Feeling pressured to comply  
- Feeling manipulated or guilt‑tripped  
- Feeling like the encounter was not random  

### **Escalation Markers**  
- Offender attempts to move deeper into victim’s life or space  
- Requests escalate from small to sensitive  
- Offender tests compliance (“Can you do me a quick favor?”)  

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Initial Access + Persistence → **embedding phase**  
- Initial Access + Privilege Escalation → **control‑building**  
- Initial Access + Lateral Movement → **expansion phase**  

---



# 📘 **DETECTION INDICATORS FOR BEHAVIORAL TACTICS**  
## **Installment 2 — Persistence, Privilege Escalation, Defense Evasion**

---

# 🟦 **4. Persistence — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is maintaining their foothold in the victim’s world.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- Offender inserts themselves into daily routines  
- Repeated “check‑ins” that feel obligatory  
- Attempts to normalize constant presence  
- Sudden interest in controlling schedules or habits  
- Offender reacts negatively when access is limited  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- Offender appears in places they shouldn’t logically be  
- Items belonging to offender appear in victim’s space  
- Signs of monitoring (notes, cameras, planted objects)  
- Repeated visits without clear purpose  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- Feeling watched or constantly evaluated  
- Feeling obligated to respond or comply  
- Feeling like the offender is “always around”  
- Difficulty setting boundaries  

---

### **Escalation Markers**  
- Offender becomes possessive of victim’s time  
- Attempts to control who victim interacts with  
- Offender becomes hostile when access is denied  
- Monitoring becomes more explicit  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Persistence + Privilege Escalation → **control consolidation**  
- Persistence + Defense Evasion → **covert embedding**  
- Persistence + Collection → **long‑term exploitation cycle**  

---

# 🟦 **5. Privilege Escalation — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is increasing their authority, influence, or control.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- Offender begins making decisions for the victim  
- Offender demands access to sensitive areas or information  
- Sudden shift from friendly to authoritative tone  
- Offender reframes relationship roles (“You owe me…”)  
- Increasingly intrusive questioning  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- Offender gains access to restricted spaces  
- Victim’s belongings are moved or searched  
- Offender appears in higher‑risk or private areas  
- Signs of forced access (doors, drawers, devices)  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- Feeling pressured to give more access  
- Feeling like the offender is “taking over”  
- Feeling coerced into decisions  
- Feeling like boundaries are being overridden  

---

### **Escalation Markers**  
- Offender demands exclusive access  
- Offender uses guilt, threats, or manipulation to gain authority  
- Offender begins controlling finances, movement, or communication  
- Offender tests compliance with increasingly bold requests  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Privilege Escalation + Credential Access → **deep infiltration**  
- Privilege Escalation + Lateral Movement → **network expansion**  
- Privilege Escalation + Impact → **pre‑harm dominance**  

---

# 🟦 **6. Defense Evasion — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is avoiding detection, accountability, or confrontation.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- Offender lies about whereabouts or actions  
- Offender deletes messages or denies conversations  
- Offender uses vague, coded, or evasive language  
- Offender shifts blame or gaslights victim  
- Offender avoids witnesses or documentation  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- Missing or altered physical evidence  
- Disabled cameras, locks, or alarms  
- Signs of tampering with victim’s belongings  
- Hidden items (weapons, notes, tools) discovered  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- Feeling confused about events  
- Feeling like reality is being rewritten  
- Feeling uncertain about their own memory  
- Feeling isolated from support systems  

---

### **Escalation Markers**  
- Offender becomes more secretive  
- Offender begins controlling victim’s access to information  
- Offender removes or destroys evidence  
- Offender threatens consequences for disclosure  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Defense Evasion + Persistence → **covert embedding**  
- Defense Evasion + Impact → **harm concealment**  
- Defense Evasion + Command & Control → **covert influence channel**  

---

## **Installment 3 — Credential Access, Discovery, Lateral Movement**

---

# 🟦 **7. Credential Access — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is attempting to obtain the “keys” to deeper access — emotional, social, or physical.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- **Repeated probing questions** about sensitive topics  
- **Attempts to bypass boundaries** (“Just tell me your password so I can help…”)  
- **Requests for access to private spaces** (rooms, devices, accounts)  
- **Emotional pressure to disclose**  
- **Coercive or forced authentication** (unlocking doors, devices under threat)  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- **Missing or moved personal items** (keys, cards, notebooks)  
- **Signs of searching** (drawers disturbed, bags rifled)  
- **Unauthorized presence** in restricted areas  
- **Repeated observation of access routines**  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- **Feeling pressured to share private info**  
- **Feeling like privacy is being invaded**  
- **Feeling monitored during sensitive actions**  

---

### **Escalation Markers**  
- **Offender begins demanding access**  
- **Offender uses threats or guilt**  
- **Offender attempts to control authentication steps**  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Credential Access + **Privilege Escalation** → **deep infiltration**  
- Credential Access + **Discovery** → **mapping + access pairing**  
- Credential Access + **Lateral Movement** → **expansion phase**  

---

# 🟦 **8. Discovery — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is mapping the victim’s world — socially, emotionally, or physically.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- **Unusual curiosity about relationships**  
- **Probing for vulnerabilities**  
- **Testing emotional boundaries**  
- **Watching routines closely**  
- **Asking about schedules or habits**  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- **Unknown individuals observing spaces**  
- **Repeated presence near entrances/exits**  
- **Objects moved or inspected**  
- **Notes, photos, or recordings found**  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- **Feeling studied or analyzed**  
- **Feeling like someone is “mapping” your life**  
- **Feeling exposed or monitored**  

---

### **Escalation Markers**  
- **Questions become more targeted**  
- **Offender begins testing access points**  
- **Offender attempts to isolate victim**  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Discovery + **Reconnaissance** → **full mapping phase**  
- Discovery + **Lateral Movement** → **expansion into new areas**  
- Discovery + **Collection** → **harvesting phase**  

---

# 🟦 **9. Lateral Movement — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is expanding their reach into new people, spaces, or systems.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- **Offender begins contacting people around the victim**  
- **Offender impersonates victim or uses their identity**  
- **Offender pressures victim to involve others**  
- **Offender uses victim as a conduit**  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- **Offender appears in new physical areas**  
- **Tools or items moved into new locations**  
- **Signs of expanding surveillance**  
- **New individuals suddenly involved**  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- **Feeling like offender is “spreading” into life**  
- **Feeling loss of control over who is involved**  
- **Feeling pressured to introduce offender to others**  

---

### **Escalation Markers**  
- **Offender gains access to new people or spaces**  
- **Offender uses victim’s identity or resources**  
- **Offender begins coordinating with accomplices**  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Lateral Movement + **Collection** → **multi‑target harvesting**  
- Lateral Movement + **Command & Control** → **distributed influence network**  
- Lateral Movement + **Impact** → **multi‑victim harm phase**

*The offender is harvesting emotional, informational, or physical value.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- **Unusual depth of questioning**  
- **Offender remembers small details too precisely**  
- **Attempts to steer conversations toward vulnerabilities**  
- **Offender watches reactions closely**  
- **Offender requests sensitive documents or items**  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- **Missing or photographed documents**  
- **Notes or recordings found**  
- **Objects moved or inspected**  
- **Signs of eavesdropping**  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- **Feeling emotionally “mined”**  
- **Feeling interrogated rather than conversed with**  
- **Feeling exposed or over‑shared**  

---

### **Escalation Markers**

- **Offender begins storing or organizing information**  
- **Offender uses collected info to influence victim**  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**

# 🟦 **11. Command & Control — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is establishing a channel to direct, influence, or control the victim.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- **Offender initiates constant communication**  
- **Offender uses guilt, fear, or obligation to maintain contact**  
- **Offender demands immediate responses**  
- **Offender uses coded or secretive communication**  
- **Offender isolates victim from alternative voices**  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- **Hidden notes, symbols, or objects used as signals**  
- **Burner phones or alternate communication devices**  
- **Repeated calls/messages from unknown numbers**  
- **Signs of remote monitoring**  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- **Feeling obligated to respond immediately**  
- **Feeling controlled through communication**  
- **Feeling anxious when not responding**  

---

### **Escalation Markers**  
- **Offender begins issuing instructions**  
- **Offender uses threats or consequences for non‑response**  
- **Offender expands communication to new channels**  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- C2 + **Lateral Movement** → **distributed influence network**  
- C2 + **Impact** → **harm‑delivery channel**  
- C2 + **Persistence** → **long‑term control loop**  

---

# 🟦 **12. Exfiltration — Detection Indicators**  
*The offender is removing emotional, informational, or physical assets from the victim.*

---

### **Behavioral Red Flags**  
- **Offender suddenly withdraws after extracting value**  
- **Offender asks for “one last detail” repeatedly**  
- **Offender pressures victim to hand over items**  
- **Offender uses secrecy around what they take**  

---

### **Environmental Indicators**  
- **Missing documents or valuables**  
- **Signs of staged removal**  
- **Unusual containers, bags, or transport items**  
- **Evidence of copying, photographing, or scanning**  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**  
- **Feeling drained or exploited**  
- **Feeling like something was “taken” emotionally**  
- **Feeling violated or exposed**  

---

### **Escalation Markers**  
- **Offender begins removing larger or more sensitive items**  
- **Offender uses threats to force handover**  
- **Offender moves items to off‑site locations**  

---

### **Cross‑Tactic Correlation Signals**  
- Exfiltration + **Impact** → **harm‑delivery phase**  
- Exfiltration + **Collection** → **harvest + removal cycle**  
- Exfiltration + **Defense Evasion** → **covert extraction**  

---

### **Victim‑State Indicators**

### **Escalation Markers**
