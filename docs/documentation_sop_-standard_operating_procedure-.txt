**DOCUMENTATION SOP (STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE)**  
*A complete, formal procedure for documenting threats or harm.*

---

# 1. **Purpose**  
This SOP establishes a standardized, defensible process for documenting threats, intimidation, harassment, or harmful behavior.  
Its goals are to:

- **Preserve accurate evidence**  
- **Enable early threat detection**  
- **Support protective‑intelligence analysis**  
- **Ensure consistent reporting across personnel**  

---

# 2. **Scope**  
This SOP applies to:

- Employees  
- Supervisors  
- Security personnel  
- HR staff  
- Contractors  
- Any individual responsible for reporting concerning behavior  

It covers **verbal threats, implied threats, intimidation, physical aggression, stalking behaviors, and property damage**.

---

# 3. **Definitions**  

### **Threat**  
A statement or action that communicates intent to cause harm.

### **Implied Threat**  
A statement or action that suggests harm without stating it directly.

### **Concerning Behavior**  
Actions that indicate potential escalation, even without a threat.

### **Evidence**  
Any physical, digital, or testimonial material relevant to the incident.

---

# 4. **Roles and Responsibilities**  

### **Reporter**  
- **Document facts objectively**  
- **Preserve evidence**  
- **Submit report promptly**  

### **Supervisor**  
- **Review documentation for completeness**  
- **Determine initial escalation**  

### **Security / Protective Intelligence**  
- **Conduct threat assessment**  
- **Initiate protective measures**  

### **HR / Administration**  
- **Ensure policy compliance**  
- **Coordinate follow‑up actions**  

---

# 5. **Procedure Overview**  
The documentation process consists of:

1. **Immediate Safety Check**  
2. **Initial Incident Capture**  
3. **Full Documentation**  
4. **Evidence Preservation**  
5. **Escalation Decision**  
6. **Submission and Storage**  
7. **Follow‑Up Actions**

Each step is detailed below.

---

# 6. **Step 1 — Immediate Safety Check**  
Before documenting:

- **Ensure the area is safe**  
- **Move victim to a secure location**  
- **Notify security if threat is active**  
- **Do not engage the offender**  

Documentation begins **only after safety is established**.

---

# 7. **Step 2 — Initial Incident Capture**  
Record immediately:

- **Date and time**  
- **Location**  
- **People present**  
- **Short factual summary**  

This prevents memory distortion.

---

# 8. **Step 3 — Full Documentation Requirements**  

### **A. Verbatim Statements**  
- **Capture exact words**  
- Use quotation marks  
- Include tone only if observable (“shouted,” “whispered”)

### **B. Physical Actions**  
- **Describe movements and gestures**  
- **Record proximity and contact**  

### **C. Environmental Context**  
- **Lighting, noise, obstacles**  
- **Presence of objects or weapons**  

### **D. Victim Impact**  
- **Record observable emotional or physical effects**  

### **E. Witness Information**  
- **Names and contact details**  

---

# 9. **Step 4 — Evidence Preservation**  
Preserve:

- **Original messages**  
- **Screenshots**  
- **Photos of damage**  
- **Voicemails**  
- **Physical objects**  

Do **not** edit, forward, or alter evidence.

---

# 10. **Step 5 — Escalation Decision**  
Use the escalation decision‑tree to determine:

- **Is this a high‑risk threat?**  
- **Is crisis response required?**  
- **Is security or HR escalation needed?**  

Escalation is mandatory when:

- Threat is specific  
- Physical contact occurred  
- Weapons are referenced or present  
- Victim expresses fear  
- Pattern of incidents exists  

---

# 11. **Step 6 — Submission and Storage**  
Submit documentation to:

- **Security**  
- **HR**  
- **Protective‑intelligence team**  

Store reports in:

- **Secure, access‑controlled systems**  
- **With chain‑of‑custody tracking**  

Never store on personal devices.

---

# 12. **Step 7 — Follow‑Up Actions**  
Follow‑up may include:

- **Security review**  
- **HR investigation**  
- **Protective‑intelligence monitoring**  
- **Victim support resources**  
- **Adjusting protective measures**  

---

# 13. **Quality Standards**  
Documentation must be:

- Accurate  
- Objective  
- Complete  
- Timely  
- Professional  
- Free of speculation  

---

# 14. **Common Errors to Avoid**  
- Adding opinions  
- Using emotional language  
- Paraphrasing threats  
- Leaving out context  
- Failing to preserve evidence  
- Delaying documentation
