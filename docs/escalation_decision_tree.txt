**Escalation Decision‑Tree**
-Built as a clear, operational flow that protective‑intelligence teams can follow under pressure.

This is designed to be:

- Fast  
- Binary (yes/no)  
- Actionable  
- Defensible  
- Easy to train and deploy  

---

# 📘 **INSTALLMENT 4 — ESCALATION DECISION‑TREE**  
*A structured, step‑by‑step escalation flow for threats or harm.*

---

# 🌲 **ESCALATION DECISION‑TREE (Behavioral Threat Documentation)**

---

# 🟦 **1. Did a threat occur?**  
**YES →** Go to **2**  
**NO →** Go to **1A**

### **1A. Was there concerning behavior without a threat?**  
- **YES →** Document as *pre‑incident behavior* and monitor.  
- **NO →** No escalation required; log only if policy mandates.

---

# 🟦 **2. Was the threat direct or implied?**  
**Direct →** Go to **3**  
**Implied →** Go to **3A**

### **3A. Does the implied threat reference harm, retaliation, or consequences?**  
- **YES →** Treat as a threat → Go to **3**  
- **NO →** Document and monitor; escalate if repeated.

---

# 🟦 **3. Was physical action involved?**  
Examples: stepping forward rapidly, blocking exits, grabbing objects.

**YES →** Go to **4**  
**NO →** Go to **3B**

### **3B. Was the threat severe or specific?**  
- **YES →** Escalate to security immediately → Go to **7**  
- **NO →** Document fully → Go to **6**

---

# 🟦 **4. Was physical contact made?**  
**YES →** Go to **5**  
**NO →** Go to **4A**

### **4A. Was the victim’s movement restricted?**  
- **YES →** Treat as high‑risk → Go to **7**  
- **NO →** Document and escalate to supervisor → Go to **6**

---

# 🟦 **5. Was there injury or property damage?**  
**YES →** Immediate escalation → Go to **7**  
**NO →** Go to **5A**

### **5A. Was the contact intentional or aggressive?**  
- **YES →** Escalate to security → Go to **7**  
- **NO →** Document and monitor → Go to **6**

---

# 🟦 **6. Does the offender have a pattern of prior incidents?**  
**YES →** Escalate to security or HR → Go to **7**  
**NO →** Document thoroughly and notify supervisor.

---

# 🟦 **7. Is there evidence of weapon involvement?**  
**YES →** Immediate crisis response → Go to **8**  
**NO →** Go to **7A**

### **7A. Did the offender reference weapons verbally?**  
- **YES →** Treat as high‑risk → Go to **8**  
- **NO →** Continue with standard escalation → Go to **9**

---

# 🟦 **8. Activate crisis‑response protocol**  
This includes:

- Security dispatch  
- Emergency services if needed  
- Evacuation or safe‑room placement  
- Immediate protective measures  

After safety is secured → Go to **9**

---

# 🟦 **9. Complete full documentation**  
Include:

- Verbatim statements  
- Physical actions  
- Environmental context  
- Evidence  
- Victim impact  
- Witness information  
- Immediate actions taken  

Then → Go to **10**

---

# 🟦 **10. Determine follow‑up requirements**  
- **Security review**  
- **HR involvement**  
- **Protective‑intelligence analysis**  
- **Victim support resources**  
- **Monitoring plan**  

If risk remains elevated → Go to **11**

---

# 🟦 **11. Initiate ongoing monitoring**  
- Track repeated behaviors  
- Update threat logs  
- Maintain communication with victim  
- Adjust protective measures  

If escalation occurs again → Return to **1** and restart the tree.

---
