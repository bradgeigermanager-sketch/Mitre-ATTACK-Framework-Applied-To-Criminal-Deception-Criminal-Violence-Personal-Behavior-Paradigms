# 📘 **DOCUMENTING THREATS OR HARM — FIELD PROTOCOL**

---

# 🟦 **1. Core Principles of Documentation**  
These are the non‑negotiables.

- **Be factual, not interpretive**  
- **Record immediately after the incident**  
- **Capture exact words and actions**  
- **Preserve original evidence**  
- **Avoid speculation or diagnosis**  
- **Document your own observations only**  

Documentation is strongest when it is **objective, time‑stamped, and specific**.

---

# 🟦 **2. What to Capture Verbatim**  
These items should be written **exactly as spoken or displayed**, using quotation marks.

- **Direct threats** (“I’m going to hurt you…”)  
- **Implied threats** (“You’ll regret this…”)  
- **Commands or coercive statements**  
- **Demanded actions**  
- **Exact phrases used during intimidation**  

If the offender wrote something (text, email, note), **preserve the original** and document:

- **Exact spelling, punctuation, and formatting**  

This prevents disputes about meaning or tone.

---

# 🟦 **3. What to Capture Objectively**  
These are **your observations**, not interpretations.

- **Physical actions** (e.g., “He stepped toward me quickly…”)  
- **Body language** (e.g., clenched fists, pacing)  
- **Environmental context** (location, lighting, who was present)  
- **Sequence of events**  
- **Injuries or damage**  
- **Victim emotional state** (without diagnosing)  

Avoid subjective labels like “angry,” “crazy,” or “violent.”  
Instead describe **observable behavior**.

---

# 🟦 **4. The Gold‑Standard Documentation Template**  
This is the format used by protective‑intelligence teams, HR investigators, and law‑enforcement liaisons.

---

## **A. Date, Time, Location**  
- Exact timestamp  
- Exact location  
- Who was present  

---

## **B. Incident Summary (Objective)**  
A short, factual description of what happened.

---

## **C. Verbatim Statements**  
Use quotation marks.  
Include tone indicators only if directly observable (e.g., “shouted,” “whispered”).

---

## **D. Physical Actions**  
Describe movements, gestures, proximity, and contact.

---

## **E. Environmental Details**  
Lighting, noise, obstacles, exits, objects used or moved.

---

## **F. Evidence Collected**  
- Photos  
- Messages  
- Voicemails  
- Damaged property  
- Witness statements  

---

## **G. Impact on Victim**  
Describe **observable** effects, not interpretations.

---

## **H. Immediate Actions Taken**  
Security notified, area evacuated, medical care sought, etc.

---

## **I. Follow‑Up Needed**  
Security review, HR involvement, protective measures.

---

# 🟦 **5. What *Not* to Include**  
These weaken the credibility of the documentation.

- **Speculation about motives**  
- **Psychological labels**  
- **Assumptions about future behavior**  
- **Character judgments**  
- **Second‑hand information without attribution**  

Stick to **what you saw, heard, or collected**.

---

# 🟦 **6. How to Store Documentation Safely**  
- **Use secure, access‑controlled systems**  
- **Preserve originals separately**  
- **Avoid personal devices**  
- **Maintain chain of custody**  

Documentation is only useful if it’s **admissible and protected**.

---

# 🟦 **7. When to Escalate Documentation**  
Escalation is warranted when:

- **Threats become specific**  
- **Violence or property damage occurs**  
- **Victim expresses fear for safety**  
- **Offender repeats threatening behavior**  
- **Weapons are mentioned or displayed**  

These are **high‑risk indicators** requiring immediate protective action.

---

# 🟦 **8. Example of a Properly Documented Threat**  
(Not real, not graphic — purely instructional.)

> **Date/Time:** May 28, 2026, 4:15 PM  
> **Location:** Loading dock, Building C  
> **Incident Summary:** Offender approached within 2 feet of victim and made threatening statements.  
> **Verbatim Statement:** “If you walk away again, I’ll make sure you regret it.”  
> **Physical Actions:** Offender stepped forward quickly, clenched fists, blocked exit path.  
> **Environmental Details:** Area was isolated; no other staff present.  
> **Impact:** Victim appeared visibly shaken, breathing rapidly.  
> **Evidence:** Security camera footage requested; victim saved text messages from earlier.  
> **Actions Taken:** Security notified; victim escorted to safe area.

This format is **defensible, clear, and actionable**.
